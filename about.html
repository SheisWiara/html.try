About Me

I’m a nursing student who spends a lot of time learning how to take care of people… and occasionally surviving exams, clinicals, and caffeine shortages. Healthcare is my main focus, but I also believe in keeping life interesting (and my brain busy), which is how I ended up falling down the rabbit hole of web development.

When I’m not studying nursing concepts, I’m learning how to build websites through Udemy. What started as a “let me just try this” hobby quickly turned into me debugging code at 2 a.m. and celebrating when a button finally works. I’m currently learning the basics of front-end and back-end development, discovering that coding requires just as much patience as nursing—maybe more.

Balancing nursing and web development has taught me how to problem-solve, stay curious, and laugh at my mistakes (especially when nothing works the first time). Whether it’s caring for patients or fixing broken code, I enjoy learning, growing, and proving that you can be both a future nurse and a future tech wizard—scrubs by day, coder by night
